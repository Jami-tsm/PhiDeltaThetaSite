    Our Cardinal Principles

    Friendship

    Great friendships are a crucial component of living well, and Phi Delta Theta challenges its members to create long-term and meaningful relationships with each other that stand the test of time.

    Sound Learning

    Sound Learning is much more than getting good grades. It’s about intellectual curiosity and the search for truth. It’s about well-roundedness and becoming cultured in all aspects of life.

    Rectitude

    Rectitude can be defined in many ways, but the Fraternity resonates with its qualities of honesty, integrity, character, kindness, and compassion.

    Our History

    After their first organization, Delta Sigma Rho, a national business fraternity, faced member disqualification due to a restructuring of the business school as well as some national issues, the members had disaffiliated and were looking for a new organization to join. In their search they had come across Phi Delta Theta, a fraternity built on three cardinal Principles: Friendship, Sound Learning, and Rectitude. These principles were exactly what they had been searching for out of and organization. And so, Phi Delta Theta was officially chartered here on RIT's campus, February 8th, 1986, with a founding class of 39 members. In 2017, our chapter was placed in escrow until our refounding in 2019. Since then, Phi Delta Theta has been helping the men of RIT become the best versions of themselves.
